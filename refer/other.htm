Full-Length Streaming Audio/Video Course Hall of Fame

This list shows shows the folks who have produced the worlds earliest examples of complete college-level courses delivered over the Internet using streaming video/audio and synchronized multimedia.
  • Stanford University began offering streaming audio/video with synchronized slides using VXtreme Winter 1995. The course was taught by Anoop Gupta.
  • EGR124 - Internet and Technology - Charles Severance - Michigan State University - June 1997 using RealPlayer
  • Hotel Restaurant Management 320 University of Delaware - Produced by Paul Rickards - January 1998 using RealPlayer
  • CSE230 - Introduction to C++ - Richard Enbody - Michigan State University - Rolled out September 1998 using RealPlayer
  • Engineering Materials - MEC 223. - These are done by Ken Ekegren - Rolled out September 1998 - North Central Technical College Mansfield, Ohio using a variety of tools including RealPresenter and Corel Presentation. Also take a look at Web Enhanced Education.
